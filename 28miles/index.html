28 for 28

I have always loved walking. Since I don't have a car, and I live in such a blessedly walkable area, I pretty much walk everywhere and can rack up the miles quickly. But I don't typically walk for sustained long distances - usually it'll be a mile or two here, a mile or two there, adding up over the course of a day.

This fall I did two long-ish day hikes in Harpers Ferry, WV with my mom, and I felt great. It reminded me how much I love to plod on, and it made me start to wonder what my limits were in terms of walking. I've always wanted to do a lengthy walk in the Greater Boston area (like Somerville to Walden Pond) and cooked up this idea to try and walk 28 miles on my 28th birthday.

It's over a marathon, and I haven't been training for it like it's a marathon, so maybe this is really stupid. But I want to at least try! I'm also trying to raise some money for Boston Food Not Bombs and for the Shamia family in Gaza.

Plan

My plan is to do this walk the day of my birthday - the 24th. If the weather is bad on my birthday, I'll do the walk on the 26th. I'm going to start walking fairly early (right before sunrise) and hope to be done by the early evening.

My route is still in the works, but in order to make it easy to take breaks and use the bathroom, I'll probably start on the Minuteman Trail and split off to the Bay Circuit Trail in Bedford. I'm also tempted to do a "city walk" but I think that will take substantially longer, as I'll have to stop at crosswalks. I'll be tracking my route/miles via Strava on the day of the walk.
